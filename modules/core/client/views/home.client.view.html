What is UQ Votes?

A democracy app: Building a bridge between you and your student representatives.
 We are not radical, and we are not undermining representative democracy at UQ. In fact, the opposite is true - we want to make representative democracy more representative.

Why does UQ Votes exist?

Democracy at the UQ is at a crossroads.
Only 28% of UQ students voted at the last UQ Union (UQU) elections. In the recent past, we have seen egregious breaches of democratic principles.
The UQU system of democracy mirrors the representative system of the Australian governments. That is, we vote for political candidates once every year, but have no real say on the issues in-between elections.
This failure to listen has led to students feeling disconnected, disillusioned and disengaged from political discourse.
Students need to be informed, engaged and empowered for democracy at UQ to truly work.

What is the UQ Union Election?

Each year the UQ Union holds an election where students can vote for various positions in the Union, Union Council and on certain committees and boards. These positions are held by students for the year following the election.

How do I vote at the UQ Union Election?

When you go to vote, you will receive three ballot papers.

  • The Executive Ballot Paper includes the student executive positions like Union President as well as other Office Bearers and positions on committees.
  • The Union Newspaper Editor Ballot is where you can vote for the team who will put together Semper Floreat.
  • Council Ballot Paper is where you can vote for a student from your faculty to represent you at meetings of Union Council.
The easiest way to vote is to head down to a polling booth with your student ID card at one of the times and locations listed below. If you have any questions or are unsure of what to do, the friendly polling clerks will be able to help you with the process.

When do I vote at the UQ Union Election?

During the week starting Tuesday 2 October 2018, the parties and candidates who have nominated to run for positions at UQ Union will campaign throughout the UQ campuses.
The polls open on Monday 8 October 2018 and close on Friday 12 October 2018.

Why should I vote at the UQ Union Election?

Voting is optional but it is the one formal chance you have in the year to decide who you want representing you - you also happen to get a $5 voucher for use at the UQ Union refectories.

Who should I vote for at the UQ Union Election?

We can't tell you that, but UQ Votes is here to help you decide how you could vote by providing you with information on the issues and the parties... So, what issues matter to you?
